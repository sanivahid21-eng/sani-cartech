Frequently Asked Questions

Why use a European specialist for my BMW, Mercedes or Audi in Ocean Grove?
European vehicles use proprietary diagnostic protocols (BMW ISTA, Mercedes XENTRY, Audi/VW ODIS) that generic OBD scanners can’t access. A European specialist has both the manufacturer-level tools and the brand-specific experience.
Do you do ECU coding in Ocean Grove?
Yes. We provide ECU coding and programming for BMW, Mercedes-Benz, Audi, Volkswagen, Porsche, Mini, Skoda and Volvo. Common coding work includes after-replacement coding, retrofit feature activation, transmission adaptation reset, and SCN coding.
How long does a typical European car diagnostic take?
A standard fault diagnosis takes between 45 minutes and two hours. Complex intermittent faults or CAN bus communication issues may require longer or a follow-up appointment.
What is the cost to replace an ECU on a European car?
Full ECU replacement typically ranges from $1,200 to $3,500+ depending on the model. Coding to the vehicle is included. In many cases, the existing ECU can be repaired or recoded rather than replaced.
Do you provide mobile diagnostic services across Ocean Grove?
Yes — fully mobile across Ocean Grove and surrounding suburbs. Initial fault diagnosis happens at your location, and coding work, ECU programming, and most module-level work also happen on-site with the equipment carried in the van.
Can you reset an airbag warning light after a collision?
Yes. We perform airbag module resets, diagnose any underlying SRS faults, and confirm the system is fully functional before signing off.
