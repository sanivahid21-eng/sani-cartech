Frequently Asked Questions

Can you service a Tesla independently in Waurn Ponds without voiding warranty?
Yes — under Australian Consumer Law, an independent EV-capable service operator can perform service work without affecting warranty, provided OEM-equivalent parts and procedures are used. Software-related faults still need to go through Tesla.
How do you check EV battery health?
EV battery health is measured as State of Health (SOH) — the current usable capacity as a percentage of original design capacity. We capture this via the vehicle’s battery management system, plus check for cell balance and any thermal events in the fault history.
What’s the hybrid battery replacement cost vs repair?
Hybrid battery replacement cost ranges $4,000–$8,000 depending on vehicle. Module-level repair is typically $1,200–$3,200 — often the better-value path, especially on Toyota and Lexus packs where individual modules can be swapped without replacing the whole pack.
Why isn’t my EV charging at full speed?
Reduced charge speed has several causes: cold battery (thermal management), reduced charge cable performance, charge port contamination, contactor wear, or onboard charger faults. Diagnostics isolate which one without replacing parts unnecessarily.
Do I need a hybrid mechanic in Waurn Ponds for routine servicing?
Not necessarily for routine items (oil changes, brake pads, cabin filters) — any competent mobile mechanic can do those. For hybrid system warnings, battery health checks, or anything HV-related, you do want a mobile operator with the right tooling and HV training.
Can you repair a hybrid showing ‘check hybrid system’?
Almost always, yes. That warning is usually a hybrid battery module fault or inverter issue. Proper diagnostics first isolate the actual cause, then quote the module-level repair — usually the cost-effective path versus full-pack replacement.
